# 🧠 3 Practical Ways to Assign Weights (in increasing maturity)

## ✅ 1. Frequency *within your target PDFs* (your current direction, but improved)

### Idea:

Words that appear **often in your 10 PDFs** → more important

But NOT raw frequency — normalize it.

### Formula:

```
weight = frequency_in_target_docs / total_docs
```

👉 Example:

* "gstin" appears in 9/10 PDFs → weight = 0.9 (HIGH)
* "invoice" appears in 5/10 → weight = 0.5
* "date" appears in 10/10 → ⚠️ but also appears everywhere → problem

👉 So this alone is **not enough**

---

## 🚀 2. TF-IDF (THIS is what you actually want)

This solves your main issue:

> “How do I know a word is *important*, not just frequent?”

### Concept:

* **TF (Term Frequency)** → how often word appears in your PDFs
* **IDF (Inverse Document Frequency)** → how rare it is across all PDFs

### Meaning:

> Important words = frequent in your type BUT rare overall

---

### 🔥 Formula:

[
\text{weight} = TF \times IDF
]

TF\text{-}IDF = TF \times IDF

---

### Example:

| Word    | In your PDFs | In general PDFs | Weight  |
| ------- | ------------ | --------------- | ------- |
| gstin   | High         | Rare            | 🔥 High |
| invoice | High         | Common          | Medium  |
| date    | High         | Very common     | ❌ Low   |

👉 This is exactly what you need.

---

## 🧪 3. Discriminative Weight (Senior-level thinking)

Now think like this:

> “Does this word HELP me distinguish my PDF from others?”

### Idea:

Compare:

* Target PDFs (your 10)
* Non-target PDFs (some random PDFs)

### Formula:

```
weight = freq_in_target - freq_in_other
```

👉 Example:

* "policy number":

  * target → 0.8
  * other → 0.1
    → weight = 0.7 ✅ strong signal

* "date":

  * target → 1.0
  * other → 1.0
    → weight = 0 ❌ useless
