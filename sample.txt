# 🥇 1. Normalized Score (VERY HIGH ROI)

### Problem in your system:

Long PDFs → more words → higher score (biased)

---

### Fix:

```python
score = matched_weight / total_words
```

or better:

```python
score = matched_weight / (total_words + 1)
```

---

### Why this is powerful:

* Removes length bias
* Makes scores comparable across PDFs

👉 This alone can improve accuracy a lot

---

# 🥈 2. Coverage-Based Scoring (VERY IMPORTANT)

Instead of just “how many matches” → also check:

> “How many UNIQUE keywords matched?”

---

### Idea:

```python
coverage = matched_keywords / total_keywords
```

---

### Example:

| Case | Matches                      | Coverage |
| ---- | ---------------------------- | -------- |
| A    | 10 hits (same word repeated) | ❌ low    |
| B    | 5 different keywords         | ✅ high   |

---

### Combine with score:

```python
final_score = 0.7 * weight_score + 0.3 * coverage
```

👉 This is **MUCH smarter than raw counting**

---

# 🥉 3. TF-Based Scoring (light version of TF-IDF)

Instead of just checking presence:

```python
score += weight * log(1 + frequency)
```

---

### Why?

* Prevents one word repeating 100 times from dominating

---

# 🏆 4. Cosine Similarity (BIG upgrade, still simple)

This is where things get **serious but still manageable**

---

### Idea:

Represent:

* Your known PDFs → as a vector
* New PDF → as a vector

Then compute similarity.

---

### Concept:

```text
Your PDFs → "average keyword vector"
New PDF → "its keyword vector"

Similarity → cosine(angle)
```

---

### Why this is powerful:

* Captures distribution, not just counts
* Much more stable than threshold rules

---

### Tools:

Use:

```python
from sklearn.feature_extraction.text import TfidfVectorizer
from sklearn.metrics.pairwise import cosine_similarity
```
